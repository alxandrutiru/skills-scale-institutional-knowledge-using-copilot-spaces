# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Engineering Managers

### Role Summary
Engineering Managers support team delivery capacity, staffing, and technical execution health across the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Assign engineers based on project needs and team capacity
- Support technical decision-making and delivery risk mitigation
- Remove team-level blockers requiring management support
- Coach engineers and maintain sustainable delivery practices

### Goals
- Maintain predictable team delivery and quality
- Improve team effectiveness and collaboration
- Balance project outcomes with long-term engineering health

### Typical Communication
- Weekly capacity and delivery syncs with PM/PdM
- Staffing and escalation updates with leadership
- Coaching and feedback conversations with engineers

---

## QA / Test Leads

### Role Summary
QA/Test Leads define test strategy and quality gates to ensure features meet acceptance and reliability expectations.

### Responsibilities
- Define test scope for unit, integration, and end-to-end coverage
- Align acceptance criteria with verifiable test scenarios
- Coordinate test execution and release readiness signals
- Track and triage defects with delivery teams

### Goals
- Reduce escaped defects and release risk
- Improve confidence in release readiness
- Keep quality expectations clear and consistent

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and quality updates in sprint/release cadence
- Defect triage sessions with PMs and developers
- Go/no-go recommendation inputs before deployment

---

## UX / Design Leads

### Role Summary
UX/Design Leads ensure solutions are usable, accessible, and aligned with product intent before and during delivery.

### Responsibilities
- Provide user flows, wireframes, and interaction guidance
- Validate designs against user and accessibility needs
- Partner with PdM/engineering on scope and UX trade-offs
- Support design QA before release

### Goals
- Improve user experience quality and consistency
- Reduce rework caused by unclear interaction requirements
- Ensure accessibility standards are addressed early

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and handoff sessions
- Acceptance criteria alignment with PdM and QA
- Release readiness checks for UX-critical features

---

## Operations / Support

### Role Summary
Operations/Support teams provide production readiness and post-release feedback needed for stable service delivery.

### Responsibilities
- Share operational requirements and known support constraints
- Prepare support teams with release notes and known issues
- Escalate customer-impacting incidents and trends
- Provide post-release feedback to improve future planning

### Goals
- Improve service stability and response effectiveness
- Reduce mean time to detect and resolve customer issues
- Ensure support readiness for each release

### Typical Communication
- Release briefings and support enablement updates
- Incident and escalation channels during critical events
- Post-release feedback loops into retrospectives

---

## How personas map to project phases

- **Initiation:** PdM, PM, Engineering Manager, key Stakeholders
- **Planning:** PM, PdM, Developers, QA/Test Lead, UX/Design Lead
- **Execution:** Developers, QA/Test Lead, PM, Engineering Manager
- **Release:** PM, QA/Test Lead, Operations/Support, Developers
- **Retrospective:** PM, PdM, Developers, QA/Test Lead, Operations/Support

## How these personas are used in the exercise
- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
